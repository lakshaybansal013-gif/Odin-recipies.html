Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti Aglio e Olio (garlic and oil) is a classic 10-minute Italian pasta dish that acts as the ultimate quick, "struggle meal" or midnight snack. It requires only a few pantry staples to create a rich, garlicky flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) dried spaghetti1 lb (450g) dried spaghetti
  • 1/2 cup good-quality extra-virgin olive oil
  • 6-8 cloves garlic, very thinly sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (or to taste)
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt (for pasta water)
  • Optional: Freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Boil Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add spaghetti and cook according to package directions until just shy of al dente.
  2. Save water:Reserve at least 1 cup of starchy pasta water, then drain the spaghetti.
  3. Cook Garlic/Chili: While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large pan over medium-low heat. Add the sliced garlic and cook slowly, stirring often, until it just begins to turn golden on the edges (avoid burning, as it will turn bitter).
  4. Add heat: Stir in the red pepper flakes and cook for another 30 seconds.
  5. Combine: Add the drained pasta directly to the pan with the garlic oil. Pour in 1/2 cup of the reserved pasta water.
  6. Emulsify: Stir and toss vigorously until the oil and water form a creamy sauce that coats the noodles. If too dry, add a splash more water.
  7. Serve: Turn off the heat. Stir in the parsley and Parmesan (if using). Serve immediately.
